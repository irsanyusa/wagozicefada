10 Witty Details From Movie Scenes That Flew Under Your Radar

We all have those favorite movies that we love to watch over and over again, but when it comes to seeing the seemingly obvious but not-so-in-your-face movie details, it’s a whole other story. Well, let’s see the most hilariously clever movie details you may have missed. Believe us when we say the Dwayne Johnson movie detail will make you laugh out loud.

Mean Girls (2004)

In Mean Girls (2004), queen bee Regina wears an “R” necklace around her neck. Cady begins wearing a “C” necklace once she has embarrassed Regina and taken her place.

A Goofy Movie (1995)

In A Goofy Movie, you can see in the diner scene that Goofy’s keychain is the Disney logo.

Everything Everywhere All at Once (2022)

In Everything Everywhere All at Once, viewers catch a glimpse of a sign twirler before Evelyn is depicted doing the same job. The Wang family encounters a sign twirler promoting pizza while en route to the IRS. In a different reality, Evelyn is subsequently shown performing the same job.

The initial Deirdre, played by Jamie Lee Curtis, has a collection of cat photographs on her desk at the IRS office. It seems that Deirdre’s affinity for feline imagery transcends various parallel universes, as the alternate version of her, characterized by having hot-dog fingers, has multiple framed cat pictures adorning the walls of the apartment she lives in together with Evelyn.

In reality, when it’s tax day for Evelyn, Deirdre is seen wearing a brace on her left wrist. However, in the universe where people have hot-dog fingers and rely more on their feet than their hands, Deirdre’s brace is positioned on her left ankle instead.

The Devil Wears Prada (2006)

The importance of Paris Fashion Week to Emily’s character in The Devil Wears Prada is subtly conveyed through her computer wallpaper, featuring the iconic Arc de Triomphe monument.

Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy (2004)

The Mexican restaurant where the women dine in Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy is named “Escupimos en su Alimento,” which translates to “We spit on your food” in English.

Walk Hard: The Dewey Cox Story (2007)

Following Dewey’s hasty decision to marry Darlene while still being married to Edith in Walk Hard: The Dewey Cox Story, he is depicted wearing both wedding rings simultaneously.

Jumanji: Welcome to the Jungle (2017)

In Jumanji: Welcome to the Jungle, Dwayne Johnson’s character avatar cannot read the map because paper beats Rock. As a result, his character, who possesses rock-like abilities, cannot interpret the map correctly.

The Matrix (1999)

While filming a scene in The Matrix, the camera’s reflection was visible in the doorknob, making it difficult to hide. To solve this issue, the filmmakers covered the camera with a coat and half-tied a tie to match Morpheus’ outfit, effectively camouflaging the camera in the scene.
