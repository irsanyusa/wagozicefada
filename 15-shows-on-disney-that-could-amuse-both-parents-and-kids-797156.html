15 Shows on Disney+ That Could Amuse Both Parents and Kids

There are endless ways to spend valuable time with your kids. Once in a while, it’s also a good choice to have them look at life through your eyes, complete with the delights you used to have in your childhood, like being raised on Disney movies and shows. It would be a joy of a ride to go through it all with your kids.

So today, Bright Side is highlighting the best shows you can watch on Disney+ with your children to have a jolly good time.

1. Togo

Togo is a movie based on the true story of a sled dog team, led by Leonard Seppala, that goes on a laborious journey of more than 700 miles to summon a life-saving serum.

2. Onward

Onward is a movie about 2 teenage elf brothers, Ian and Barley Lightfoot, who are out on a magical crusade to spend one more day with their deceased father. Speaking of magic, their journey is full of cryptic maps, tough hindrances, and extraordinary discoveries.

3. Shop Class

Shop Class is an interesting game show where teams of creative students have to accomplish tasks based on designing, building, and testing new inventions. The show is intriguing enough to keep you and your kids entertained.

4. Aladdin

Aladdin is a classic movie that centers around a lovable street urchin named Aladdin. He stumbles upon a magic oil lamp while visiting the palace that reveals a larger-than-life genie. Their adventures together provide pure entertainment for both kids and adults alike. There are both classic-animated and live-action versions of this movie too.

5. Dumbo

Dumbo is a movie about a special elephant who has oversized ears and can fly. A few wicked men misuse his powers for their own gain. When the circus plans a new venture, Dumbo and his friends unleash many of the deeper secrets ahead.

6. Prop Culture

If you and your children are a fan of Disney movies and have always been curious about how the magical moments are created with props and artifacts, Prop Culture is a series you must watch. It also features the many craftspeople who made them, actors who worked with them, and the collectors who have been cherishing them over the years.

7. Encore!

Encore! is a musical reality TV show where executive producer Kristen Bell brings together the cast of a high school production to perform the show again years later under the guidance of Broadway directors, choreographers, and voice coaches.

8. The World According to Jeff Goldblum

Documentaries don’t have to be boring anymore. The World According to Jeff Goldblum is a doc series that chooses a familiar object and unleashes a world of surprising connections, science, and a lot of big ideas to leave you and your kids stupified.

9. Pick of the Litter

Pick of the Litter is a heartfelt journey of 6 dogs who are out on a crusade to become guides for the Guide Dogs for the Blind training program, an extraordinary canine career.

10. The Emperor’s New School

The Emperor’s New School is an animated series about young Kuzco who anticipates becoming an emperor of the Inca Empire, but in order to do that, he must pass every class and graduate from Kuzco Academy. When he gets banished from the palace, he realizes the value of having friends and family in life. The series is a sequel to the movie, The Emperor’s New Groove.

11. Mulan

The live-action Mulan was a highly anticipated movie this year, but its animated version is equally hilarious and fun to watch with your kids. Mulan pretends to be a man to take her father’s place in the Imperial Army to save China.

12. Fresh Off the Boat

Fresh Off the Boat is based in the ’90s after an immigrant Taiwanese family moves to the US and learns to adapt to the reality of living the American dream. The series is a lighthearted comedy to watch with your kids and would also help them learn things about the Chinese culture and family values.

13. Be Our Chef

Be Our Chef is another reality show based on cooking competitions between families from various backgrounds as they prepare dishes from their own cultures.

14. Elephant

Elephant is a movie that centers around the journey of African elephant Shani and her son Jomo who attempt to travel across the Kalahari desert. This feature film is dubbed by Meghan, the Duchess of Sussex.

15. The Imagineering Story

If you’ve always been fascinated by the world of Disney and have been curious about its history and the creation of Walt Disney theme parks all over the world, then The Imagineering Story is a must-watch for you and your kids.

Bonus: Disney shorts

In the past, you may have been lucky enough to catch a few animated shorts in theaters before the beginning of a Disney movie. But now, Disney+ plus has all of its short movies in one place for you and your kids to enjoy.
